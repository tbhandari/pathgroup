Thank you for visiting our website. The practice philosophy of Path Group of Atlanta is to ensure that patients and their families receive the most comprehensive, empathic and contemporary treatment in a safe, comfortable and confidential environment. Our priority is providing attentive care in the most effective manner possible.

Making informed decisions about the course of treatment is essential to us at Path Group of Atlanta. We want our patients and their families to fully understand the potential benefits, risks, and alternatives to all proposed treatment recommendations.

The opportunity for a collaborative treatment team is essential to Path Group of Atlanta. Sound treatment decisions will be made as a team, with the patient being the most important team member. The participation of family members, primary care providers, teachers and therapists is welcomed. Please explore our website to learn more about our practice and our qualifications.

Nnenna Akaronu, M.D.

Dr. Nnenna Akaronu completed her undergraduate studies at North Carolina State University, graduating with a Bachelor of Science in Biological Sciences. She then earned her Doctor of Medicine degree from the Brody School of Medicine at East Carolina University. After graduating from medical school, Dr. Akaronu completed her residency in General Adult Psychiatry at Wake Forest Baptist Medical Center. She pursued advanced fellowship certifications in both Child and Adolescent Psychiatry at Baylor College of Medicine and Forensic Psychiatry at Emory University School of Medicine. Her leadership capabilities have been consistently recognized throughout her career, and she has demonstrated extensive mentorship of students and trainees during her medical career. Dr. Akaronu is board-certified in General Adult Psychiatry and board-eligible in both Child and Adolescent Psychiatry and Forensic Psychiatry. Her range of experience is broad, spanning inpatient, outpatient, consultation-liaison, and emergency settings, where she has utilized the most up-to-date, evidence-based practices to treat psychiatric disorders in children, adolescents, and adults. She is a dedicated educator who has presented at regional and national conferences and contributed to peer-reviewed journal publications, ensuring her clinical practice is informed by the latest advancements in the field.

Dr. Akaronu is Nigerian-American and spent her formative years in West Africa before immigrating to the United States. As such, she possesses a unique, firsthand understanding of the complexities of cultural identity, acculturation, and well-being. A perspective that provides insight into the critical developmental stages of identity formation that children and adolescents navigate. This perspective enriches her clinical practice, allowing her to connect with patients from all backgrounds with empathy and insight. Dr. Akaronu has a long-standing commitment to culturally competent care with cultural humility at the forefront. Dr. Akaronu also believes in an individualized and collaborative approach to treatment that considers a person's unique history and background, cultural influences and preferences, and their personal goals. This enables a comprehensive treatment plan that incorporates psychotherapeutic modalities, behavioral modification, and psychopharmacology tailored to each individual's specific needs.

Dr. Akaronu is accepting and offers in-person visits for new patients. For established patients in her practice, Dr. Akaronu offers in-person and telepsychiatry (video-conference) appointments.

Tyler Finklea, Ph.D.

Tyler Finklea, Ph.D. received her doctorate in Combined Counseling and School Psychology from Florida State University. While there she conducted research on the intersection of mental health and career difficulties. She completed an APA Accredited internship at American University in Washington, D.C., followed by an APA Accredited postdoctoral fellowship with Emory University’s Counseling and Psychological Services. She has worked at four university counseling centers, including as a staff psychologist at Georgia Institute of Technology.

She believes in the potential for growth and strives to walk alongside her clients to help them heal the past and live more fully in the present. Therapy may explore how one’s past is influencing the present, may focus on a concern in the present that needs immediate attention, or could be a combination of the two. She collaborates with her clients to identify these needs and goals throughout the process of therapy.

She approaches therapy through an integrative lens, including psychodynamic, relational, and mindfulness teachings and practices to work with clients to address a wide range of concerns. She especially finds working with adolescents and adults on identity development at all stages of life including LGBTQ+ identity development, anxiety, trauma, family of origin concerns, and life transitions to be fulfilling.

Trevor Strickland, APC, NCC

Trevor Strickland, MS received his degree in Clinical Mental Health Counseling from Georgia State University. He completed his practicum and internship at the Decatur High School Student Center, where he primarily worked with adolescents and their families in groups and individual sessions on a variety of issues, including but not limited to: anxiety, depression, stress, trauma, adjustment disorders, developmental delays, mood and personality disorders, social isolation, addiction, crisis and suicide intervention, gender identity and sexuality, and other mental health diagnoses with varying severity. He is certified and recognized as a Nationally Certified Counselor (NCC) by the National Board for Certified Counselors. As an Associate Professional Counselor (APC), he works under the supervision of Dr. Elana Zimand while working towards licensure as an LPC. He is trained and certified in ASSIST and Suicide-2-Hope (suicide interventions).

He believes that everyone suffers in some way or another, and our lives are in a constant state of flux. Everything changes on its own at some point, but we can learn to steer change in a way that benefits us and those around us by better understanding how we respond to suffering. He utilizes mindfulness practices, CBT skills, and a variety of other interventions to help clients see their problems clearly and create tangible plans for overcoming them. Children who are faced with adversity or struggle within their contexts (i.e. family relationships, peer relationships, academic struggles) benefit from exposure to fundamental life experiences that directly improve their developmental and emotional growth.

Therapy focuses on shifting our perception of problems so that the problem becomes an opportunity for growth and connection. For that shift to happen, we must develop non-judgemental awareness of our habitual reactions to suffering. We also learn to let go of preconceived notions about who we are and how we think life should be. Learning to let go allows us to gain clear insights into our struggles and failures, and improves our ability to accept and move through our problems. He believes there are three ways of learning - faith, intellectual investigation, and direct experience. Practice and exposure to the direct experience of skills and concepts are emphasized, especially when working with children. Once new skills are learned and practiced, we can begin to discover new ways of responding to old issues, moving into new possibilities that align with our deepest values.

New Patient Inquiry

Thank you for your interest in Path Group of Atlanta! In order to best help you, we ask that you please fill out our New Patient Inquiry form to begin the new patient process.

Our doctors have different sets of skills and expertise; please answer the form’s questions in order to assist us in providing you with a clinically appropriate match. (Please note that filling out this questionnaire does not guarantee an appointment.)

Once we have your completed form and have determined the best fit for you, we will reach out to the email address you provided in the form to initiate the scheduling process. (Please allow us at least 24-48 business hours to reply. If you have not received your email, be sure to check your Spam folder to ensure that it did not end up there.) We will go over our practice guidelines with you and work with you to schedule your initial visit. We will also set you up with an account on our patient portal, where you will be able to fill out the new patient paperwork and set up your billing information. (Please note that all paperwork must be completed prior to the appointment.)

In the case that we are not the best fit for you, we will try to provide you with other recommendations and referrals. If there is a specific question on the form that you do not feel comfortable answering, please let us know. Everything in the questionnaire is confidential and would only be shared in accordance with HIPAA and confidentiality laws.

FAQs
  • Who will we see for our medication? If you are interested in a consultation for medication, you or your child will be seen by a physician with specialized training in psychiatry. Because psychiatrists are medical doctors, they are uniquely qualified to provide psychiatric assessment and treatment while taking into account physical health and underlying medical conditions. You can be confident that the best treatment options will be offered throughout your care.

  • What are your office hours? Our office is open to assist with scheduling and general questions Monday to Friday from 9:00 am-5:00 pm. This may differ from our clinicians, who can have extended hours for patient care.

  • What if I need office assistance outside of office hours? Please call our office at 404.235.5982 and leave a voicemail with your name, contact information, and inquiry clearly stated. You may also email the office at office@pathgroupatl.com. Voicemails and emails will be reviewed the next business day during regular office hours.

  • What if I/my child/loved one needs emergency assistance? Our clinicians do not provide any crisis care or emergent medical or psychiatric services. Please contact 911 or visit the nearest hospital emergency department in case of an emergency. You can also contact the Georgia Crisis and Access line at 1-800-715-4225, which provides 24/7 access to mental health services.

  • How can I request a medication refill? Medication refill requests are submitted through our website by finding the "Patient Forms" tab on our menu at the top right and choosing the "Medication Refill Request form" option. If you do not have access to the internet, alternatively, you can call the office at 404.235.5982 and leave a message with the name of the medication, dose, quantity, and the pharmacy name along with the phone number.
    Refill requests do not constitute an emergency during non-business hours. Please be mindful that you have enough medication during weekends and holidays. Please note that we do not respond to pharmacy requests for refills as they are often automated/inaccurate.

  • Can I email my clinician? Each clinician has their own email policy, which can be discussed at your appointment. Generally, it is requested that emails sent to your clinician are for brief questions only. Extensive emails will require an appointment.
    Please note: Emails are not regularly checked after hours, weekends, and holidays.

  • Can I call my clinician? Each clinician has their own telephone policy, which can be discussed at your appointment. Phone calls are reserved for urgent questions during non-business hours.

  • Where is the office located? We are located at 550 Pharr Rd NE, in suite 605.

  • Where can I park for my appointment? There is an underground garage with elevator access that is free of charge. After parking, take the elevator to the 6th floor for suite 605 - which is directly to the right of the elevators when exiting them.

  • Do you accept insurance? Our practice does not accept any forms of health insurance. Our scheduling platform will auto-generate and email you a monthly superbill (which will include all appointments within that month) if you would like to file for possible reimbursement with your insurance's out-of-network benefits.

  • What are your fees? Our appointment fees vary depending on the age of the patient and whether they are requesting psychiatry or therapy services. Please contact the office directly for our updated fees.

  • Do you see patients outside of Georgia? Our physicians can only provide care for patients in Georgia at the time of all appointments (including phone and telehealth). Medications will only be prescribed to a pharmacy in Georgia. We highly encourage new patients who will not be in Georgia for the whole year to find a clinician in the state where they reside/go to school.

  • How often do I need an appointment with my psychiatrist? Appointment frequency is based on several factors, including the type of medication a patient is currently on. Follow-up appointments are generally every three months, though this frequency may vary due to factors such as medication changes. Patients must be seen in person at the office at least once a year.

  • Do you do any screening/testing (i.e., for ADHD)? In terms of evaluations/testing, we do not conduct any formal testing during the new patient appointment. During the initial appointment, the doctor will ask questions about your psychiatric and medical history (similar to a PCP appointment). They may ask other questions about your background like your school, work, and relationship history. If appropriate, the diagnosis of ADHD may be made, as well as a recommendation to start medication. Sometimes the doctor may diagnose something else and/or recommend something else. Together, you may decide that other testing is needed (bloodwork, psychological testing), and the appropriate next steps will be discussed.

  • What is your cancellation policy? Your scheduled appointment is reserved for you and only you. We do not overbook. Therefore, we have a strict 24-hour cancellation policy. If you are unable to keep your scheduled appointment, please call or email at least 24 hours in advance to cancel or reschedule your appointment. Please keep in mind that our schedule can fill up quickly.

    If you do not cancel your appointment more than 24 hours in advance or if you fail to show for your appointment, you will be responsible for the full cost of the appointment.
